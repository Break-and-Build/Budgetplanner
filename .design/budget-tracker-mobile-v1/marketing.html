The problem

Most budget apps stop working after week one.

You install the app full of intention. You set up categories, enter income, plan it out. You feel organized. Then real life happens. You forget. Two weeks later you have no idea where you stand — and opening the app would be admitting that.

What you actually need is one number: can I spend right now?

Most apps make this worse. They're built to keep you engaged with noise — daily streaks, push notifications celebrating $4 saved on coffee, dashboards full of charts that don't help you decide if you can afford lunch.

Budget Planner is built around the opposite instinct: one quiet answer, fast, every day.

What it does

Three screens. That's the whole product.

Open the app, see the number, decide, close the app. The full loop takes less than a minute.

01 — Home

Today's safe-to-spend

One big number at the top. Four category bars underneath. The last few things you logged. That's the whole home screen.

02 — Fast log

Three taps, five seconds

You're at the coffee shop. Tap the + at the bottom. Type the amount. Hit Log. Done. The bar moving is the only confirmation you need.

03 — Month-end

A quiet reflection

On the 28th, three soft questions: where was it tight, where did it feel flexible, what was intentional. Then the plan rolls forward.
