# Budget Planner

**A budget you actually live in.**

The plan you made on the 1st? It's still here on the 20th. Quietly tracking. Showing you what's left to spend today. No streaks. No "great job!" No bright colors yelling at you about your coffee habit.

Just the truth about your money, in your pocket.

---

## Why most budget apps stop working after week one

You install the app full of intention. You set up categories, enter your income, plan it out. You feel organized.

Then real life happens. You forget. The app sits unopened. Two weeks later you have no idea where you stand, and opening the app would be admitting that.

Most budget apps make this worse. They're built to keep you engaged with **noise** — daily streaks, push notifications celebrating $4 saved on coffee, dashboards full of charts that don't help you decide if you can afford lunch.

What you actually need is one number: *can I spend right now?*

That's what Budget Planner is built around.

---

## What it does, in three screens

### **Home: today's safe-to-spend**

One number. Big. Right at the top.

Underneath it: four category bars showing what's left in your **Essentials, Growth, Stability, and Rewards** buckets. Below those: the last few things you logged.

That's the whole home screen. No notifications nagging you. No "you spent 12% more on dining than usual." Just the answer to *what can I spend today.*

### **Fast log: three taps, five seconds**

You're at the coffee shop. You tap the **+** at the bottom of the screen. The amount field opens with the number pad already up. Your last-used category is already selected. You type the amount, hit Log, done.

The bar animates as the money moves out of the bucket. That's the only confirmation — no toast, no "great choice!" The bar moving is enough.

### **Reflection at month's end**

On the 28th, a quiet prompt appears: *"May is winding down — close out and start the next month?"*

Three soft questions: where was it tight, where did it feel flexible, what was intentional. You write what you want, skip what you don't. Then the plan rolls forward. Next month is ready.

That's the rhythm. **Plan. Live. Reflect. Repeat.**

## What we deliberately left out

| What | Why |
|---|---|
| ❌ Streak counters | You're an adult. Budgeting isn't Duolingo. |
| ❌ Daily push spam | We send **two** notifications, ever. 9am check-in. Month-end reminder. That's it. |
| ❌ Bank account linking (for now) | Manual logging takes 3 taps. The privacy + simplicity trade is worth it. |
| ❌ Charts that "celebrate" you | One progress arc, when you tap a category. No dashboards. |
| ❌ Emoji, mascots, encouragement | The interface stays still. The numbers move. |
| ❌ A score or a "financial health rating" | Money isn't a test. You aren't being graded. |

## What it actually feels like

Open the app. See the number. Decide. Close the app.

Some days the number is comfortable. Some days it's tight. The app doesn't judge either way — it just shows you the truth so you can choose what to do with it.

That's the whole product.
